SECURED VS UNSECURED

Things that are Secured are Typically backed up with either cash, a House or a Car. Things that are Secured get a larger limit and a lower APR. For a Loan this means A bigger Loan.

Bank products that are UNSecured Usually have a smaller limit and higher APR

EXAMPLE
- An Auto Loan is a loan Secured with a Car. Thats Why the APR is usually low.
- A Mortgage is a loan backed up by a house.
- A Secured Credit is a Credit Card backed up by cash.

Fixed Rate Vs. Variable Rate

A Fixed Rate means that the APR cannot change. Even if the Economy crashes the interest rate will not change.

A Variable Rate Means the Rate can change with the Economy.

Credit Card

A Credit Card is a Line of credit that consumers can Utilize to Purchase Goods or Services. Credit Card's have a variable rate.

Usage:
- It can be used to increase ones credit.
- It can be used as a rewards card(Only some CC offer rewards)
- It can be used to transfer a balance.

People do a Balance Transfer because most Credit Cards offer a period of time where the card is 0%. This Helps a lot because the individual saves money on Interest.

Loans

A Loan is a way to borrow set amount of money. unlike a line of credit you only get money from a loan once. At the time of signing a loan the consumers knows exactly how much they are paying back. A loan is Fixed.

Loans Typically has a Lower APR.

Checking Account

A checking account is a bank account for everyday expenses. It tends to have a lower interest rate than a savings or CD account, but unlike those accounts there's no limit to how often you can access your money.

Savings Account

Savings accounts are a safe place to store cash and can earn around 1% to 2% interest. They can also help you save for longer-term needs

If your not going to use your money for a long period of time you may want to consider a Certificate of deposit account for a higher return.

Heloc

A Heloc is a line of credit that is secured by a mortgage.

Refinance

Refinance is a way to renegotiate a mortgage. If one doesn't like their current APR or term they can Refinance to change it.
